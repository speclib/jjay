# Spawn Got Verbs. Now I Spawn Thinking, Not Just Work.

`jjay spawn` only ever did one thing: drop an agent on an existing change and tell it to apply. Useful — but when my main window is busy untangling a conflict, I couldn't kick off a new idea without waiting. No more.

`spawn` takes a verb now. `spawn apply <change>` is the old job, namespaced. `spawn proposal "<whatever's on your mind>"` is the new one — it spins up an isolated workspace and sets an agent loose exploring or drafting a proposal, with no change name required, because there isn't one yet. I name it myself, instantly, with plain code: I chew the prompt down to a short readable slug and that becomes the handle — `prop-dark-mode` — for good. No waiting on an AI to name a window. Explore isn't a separate verb; it's just how a proposal starts, and it grows up into a real proposal without ever being renamed. Apply spawns wear `app-`, proposals wear `prop-`, so `status` splits them into two clean tables.

Everything's isolated — every spawn gets its own workspace. Yes it's heavier; I don't care. It means two agents can think and write at once without clawing at the same files, and it keeps my merge the one true gate. And I killed the bare `spawn <change>` form on the way out — one user, pre-1.0, no patience for two spellings of the same thing.

Now the main window solves conflicts and I send the new thinking somewhere else. That's the whole point of a flock.
